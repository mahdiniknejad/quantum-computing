Some Solutions for Quantum Sensing

Ion Gyroscope Control: Since the sensitivity of atomic interferometry gyroscope (AIG) for the same envelope area compared to ring laser gyroscope (RLG) is in order of $10^{11}$ because of the mass of atom wrt photon. But the fact is that the envelope area created by atom motion around a closed path is very less wrt to ring laser gyroscopes or fiber optic gyroscopes. To overcome this drawback, we can manipulate the motion of atom clouds around the interference path to increase the area or use a larger momentum transfer based on Bragg diffraction in specified $\Lambda$-type atomic clouds. Another approach is to use ions for gyroscopic purposes instead of atoms because we can control the motion by electric or magnetic field much time rotate around to reach and even achieve higher precision than best mechanical high precise gyroscopes. In an ionic interferometry gyroscope (IIG). Synchronous to atom (ion) manipulation several times to increase area, we coherently control the quantum state of atom (ion) to implement the atom interferometer device at predefined times, it must be measured weakly via laser probe the atomic phase different which is a function of the rotation rate of the inertial navigation system. To realize the ion mirror (reflector) and ion beam-splitter elements of an interferometer, we can use traditional $\pi , \frac{\pi}{2}$ laser pulses or we will employ the quantum control pulses optimally to realize these unitary gates with high. Based on a newly devised torus-shaped trap and precise classical magnetic field control of two branches of ions, it can rotate the ion in a certain circle multiple times in opposite directions in two separate tori, and in time T with n rotation manipulate quantum coherently the ions reflectively and then in time 2T of 2n rotation, coherently realize via laser pulses the ion beams to detect the phase differences to measure rotation rate. This system is an ultra-high sensitive gyroscope more than the best mechanical gyroscopes.

Atom Interferometer: Atom Interferometer has two quantum unitary gates that must be realized for quantum sensing purposes such as atomic gravimeter and atomic interferometer gyroscope. An optimal cost function that defines the distance between a unitary mirror (M) and beam-splitter (BS) operators are defined and based on it, general adaptive (GADA) algorithms for optimization-based quantum control are innovated to realize the atomic mirror and atomic beam-splitter gates in-spite of recoil effects. For the realization of those atom interferometer gates, our approach is optimal quantum control to achieve high fidelity. The target unitary matrices for atomic mirror and beam splitter are as:

$ U_{M}=\begin{pmatrix} 0&&1\\ 1&&0 \end{pmatrix} \ \quad , \quad U_{BS}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\begin{pmatrix} 1&&i\\ i&&1 \end{pmatrix} $

The objective cost which is minimized is as: $ J_{gate}(T):=1-\frac{1}{d^2}\Big|\langle U_{gate} , U_{atom}(T)\rangle\Big|^2=1-\frac{1}{d^2}\Big|\mathrm{Tr}\big(U_{gate}^{\dagger} U_{atom}(T)\big)\Big|^2 $

Mach-Zehnder-based Atom Interferometer in space-time coordinate: At times $t_0$ and $2T+t_0$, the atomic beam splitter (BS) and also at time $T+t_0$, the atomic mirror (M) must be implemented.

Axion Dark-Photon Detection: One possible experiment to observe the dark photons is the interaction of dark photons with electron spins in a meta-stable excited state. For this purpose, a hybrid quantum control strategy must be developed as we see in the following figure which is a simultaneous mixture of coherent control and measurement-based control. With coherent light we control the electron transition from ground stable state to meta-stable and simultaneously with a classical feedback loop, we must control the electron spin to the desired spin to interact during the interaction phase with dark photon and then control to another stable level to observe the spin change for further processing.
